The history of Benz cars traces back to the creation of the first practical automobile, the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, by Karl Benz in 1886. This groundbreaking invention marked the beginning of the automotive era, with Benz eventually merging with Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft in 1926 to form Mercedes-Benz, a renowned luxury automobile brand.

The history of Chrysler cars dates back to the founding of the Chrysler Corporation by Walter P. Chrysler in 1925. The company quickly gained prominence for innovative engineering and design, introducing iconic models such as the Chrysler Airflow in the 1930s. Over the years, Chrysler has been known for producing a diverse range of vehicles, from luxury sedans to minivans, with notable periods of success and challenges, including partnerships with other automakers. Chrysler has evolved through mergers and acquisitions and is currently a part of Stellantis, a global automotive group formed in 2021.

The history of Ford cars is synonymous with automotive innovation and mass production. Founded in 1903 by Henry Ford and a group of investors, the Ford Motor Company revolutionized the industry with the introduction of the Model T in 1908. Henry Ford's groundbreaking use of assembly line techniques in 1913 not only streamlined production but also made automobiles more affordable for the general public.
